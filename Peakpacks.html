About the Project

This project tackles growing waste in mountain regions by promoting eco-friendly disposal through biodegradable trash bags used by trekkers and tourists. Waste is sorted, shredded, and processed via energy recovery methods like gasification, anaerobic digestion, and incineration. The result: reduced plastic pollution, cleaner trails, and sustainable tourism. Local jobs are created, awareness is spread, and nature is protected for generations to come.

Impact on Society

This project makes a real difference by tackling the growing problem of waste in our mountains. By using biodegradable trash bags for trekkers and tourists, it ensures that garbage is not left behind to pollute nature. Waste collected is turned into energy or safely processed, reducing harm to the environment. Local communities benefit through jobs in waste management and awareness campaigns. With cleaner trails, healthier ecosystems, and more responsible tourism, the project supports a greener future and connects people with sustainability.
